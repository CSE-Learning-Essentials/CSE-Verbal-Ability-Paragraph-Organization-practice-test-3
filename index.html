CIVIL SERVICE EXAM PRACTICE TEST

CREATED BY: CSE LEARNING ESSENTIALS

SECTION 1: VERBAL ABILITY

PART 5: PARAGRAPH ORGANIZATION

PRACTICE TEST 1 | 5-ITEM TEST | PASSING SCORE: 4

Direction: Determine which may be the best order in which the given sentences should be arranged to result in a well-organized paragraph.

1. Sentences:
I. The Department of Health launched a campaign promoting regular health checkups.
II. Early detection of diseases can significantly improve treatment outcomes.
III. Many people tend to visit the doctor only when symptoms become severe.
IV. The campaign aims to raise awareness about preventive healthcare.

2. Sentences:
I. The rise of e-books has transformed the publishing industry.
II. Digital formats allow readers to carry thousands of books on a single device.
III. Some traditional publishers are struggling to adapt to the change.
IV. However, many still prefer the feel of printed books.

3. Sentences:
I. Many cities are investing in bike lanes and pedestrian paths.
II. These infrastructure changes encourage people to choose eco-friendly modes of transport.
III. Heavy traffic and air pollution are major problems in urban areas.
IV. Choosing to walk or bike helps reduce carbon emissions and improve public health.

4. Sentences:
I. Online learning platforms have become more popular in recent years.
II. Many students find online classes flexible and convenient.
III. The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated the shift to remote education.
IV. However, some learners struggle with lack of face-to-face interaction.

5. Sentences:
I. Renewable energy sources are gaining popularity worldwide.
II. They help reduce dependence on fossil fuels and lower greenhouse gas emissions.
III. Many governments are offering incentives to promote clean energy.
IV. This transition is crucial for combating climate change.

Answer Explanations:

  1. A. I-IV-II-III
    Starts with the launch of the campaign → states its purpose → explains the benefit of early detection → contrasts with common behavior of late doctor visits.
  2. E. I-II-III-IV
    Begins with the impact of e-books → describes benefits of digital formats → mentions struggle of traditional publishers → ends with the preference for print books.
  3. A. III-I-II-IV
    Introduces urban problems → explains city investments → states the intent of encouraging eco-friendly transport → highlights the benefits.
  4. A. III-I-II-IV
    Highlights the pandemic’s effect → describes rise of platforms → mentions advantages → ends with drawbacks.
  5. E. I-II-III-IV
    Describes the trend → explains benefits → cites government incentives → emphasizes fighting climate change.
